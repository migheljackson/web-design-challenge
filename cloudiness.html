Latitude and Cloud Cover  

This visualization shows cloud cover observations as they relate to distance from the equator.  Like humidity, despite the differences in season, the visualization looks fairly well distributed.  It appears that the majority of cities on the day of this observation actually had clear skies.               

Since cloud cover can be a somewhat subjective measure, it is possible that there is some bias in the observations that is making so many of them skew towards either 0% or 80% and up.
